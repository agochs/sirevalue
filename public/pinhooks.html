Which stallions actually deliver pinhook profits?

This page ranks stallions by realized hip-level pinhook performance — same individual horse, matched across yearling and 2YO sales by sire, dam, and foal year. No averaging across cohorts: every dollar of yearling cost and every dollar of 2YO sale is from the same horse.

Indexed across — yearling hips and — 2YO hips from major U.S. sales 2022–2026.

How this works · click to expand

What this page shows. Every stallion with at least one realized pinhook pair (same horse sold as both yearling and 2YO). Ranked by median return % across all of that stallion's matched pairs. The top panel highlights the 10 single biggest pinhook returns ever recorded across the dataset.

How "matched pairs" work. We fingerprint each hip by (sire, dam, foal year). When the same fingerprint appears in both a yearling sale and a 2YO sale, we have a realized pinhook — same horse, two transactions ~18 months apart. We use the priciest sale on each side as the canonical transaction.

Trend column. If a stallion has at least 2 cohorts of foals each with n≥3 pairs, we compare the oldest cohort's median return to the newest cohort's. Differences ≥ 30 points get a ↗ rising or ↘ falling tag; in between is → stable.

How matching works. A horse foaled in year F is sold as a yearling in F+1 and as a 2YO in F+2. We fingerprint each hip by (normalized sire, normalized dam, foal year) — biologically unique within a cohort because mares only produce one foal per year. When the same fingerprint appears in both a yearling sale and a 2YO sale, we have a realized pinhook pair. RNA hips and online dispersals are excluded; canonical (highest-priced) transactions are kept when a horse appears in multiple sales.
