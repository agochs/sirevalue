First-Crop Sire Predictions

Predicted pinhook signal for stallions without data yet.

These stallions don't appear on the realized pinhook leaderboard because their first foals are too young to have completed a yearling-to-2YO sale cycle. We project their expected pinhook performance by leaning on their closest sire-line comparators (typically half-brothers — same sire, different dams) that DO have realized data.

This is a forecast, not a measurement. Confidence is high when there are several comparators with substantial sample sizes and low when we're working with thin pedigree information.

How this works. For each first-crop stallion we pull their similar-stallion list (built from shared sire, damsire, sire-line, and price tier). We keep similars that have ≥5 realized pinhook pairs in the database, take the top 8, and sample-size-weight their median return %, mean return %, and profitable %. Half-brothers (same sire, different dam) are the strongest comparator type — Kingsbarns and Arabian Knight share a comparator pool of Uncle Mo sons because they ARE Uncle Mo sons. Confidence: high = ≥4 comparators and ≥60 total pairs; medium = ≥2 comparators and ≥20 pairs; low below that.
